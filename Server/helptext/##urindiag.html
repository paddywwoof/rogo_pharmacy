The patient has indicated that there is an established or known diagnosis to explain current symptoms. To identify this diagnosis and to ascertain how much you wish to rely on this diagnosis you may want to ask the following 4 questions

  1. What is the diagnosis

  2. Which professional made the diagnosis

  3. In which clinic or organisation does this professional works

  4. When was the diagnosis made

‘What is the diagnosis’

Possible appropriate answers to this question are as follows

Urine_Infection_or_Cystitis,

Stones_in_the_bladder_or_kidney,

Sexually_Transmitted_Infections,

Prostate_problems_#_men_only,

Overactive_bladder

Uterine_prolapse_#_women_only

Following_an_operation

Any_other_reason_-_details

‘Which professional made the diagnosis’

Possible appropriate answers to this question are as follows

GP_or_GP_Nurse_Practitioner

Out_of_Hours_Dr_or_Out_of_Hours_Nurse_Practitioner

Accident_and_Emergency_Department_Doctor_or_Nurse_Practitioner

Specialist_hospital_doctor

Pharmacist

Paramedic

Dentist

‘When was this diagnosis made’

Possible appropriate answers to this question are as follows

Within_the_last_week

Within_the_last_month

Within_the_last_year

More_than_1_year_ago
